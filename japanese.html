Addendum

After having studied Japanese for little over half a year now, I have been asked by a handful of people for resources that I have been using for my self-study. When this question was first asked of me, I came to to the realization that I did not have a compiled list. This list is surely not a complete list of all useful resources out there, but it does contain the ones that I have found useful in some regard.

Have a useful resource that is missing from this list? Please reach out and share your experience!

Kana

Note about Kana.

Rating Name Tags Description
Tofugu Hiragana Guide Free! Uses mneumonics to teach Hiragana in a matter of hours.
Tofugu Katakana Guide Free! The same method of mneumonics but for Katakana.
Hiragana Drag and Drop Drill Free! (Flash) Good hands-on practice as you learn each row of the table.
Hiragana Flashcards Free! Another self-paced drill, recommend enabling all fonts in 'extras' for more reading practice.

Ratings

Vital Represent the core resources of my daily routine or that provide tremendous value to learners. These are the items that I feel are fundamental to progressing your knowledge.
Effective Denote content I occassionally use (or have previously used) to supplement my personal studies. These items might be essential for others, but may not necessarily be a part of what I use.
Useful Identify items that I infrequently access or don't use at all, but acknowledge their value as alternative for others to benefit from using. Everyone learns differently, so having extra options is always a plus!
Unevaluated Contains links and resources that I have not personally evaluated or used, but want to provide to give other users a chance to explore for themselves.
